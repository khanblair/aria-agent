# Phase: SCAN — Exploring Problem Domains

**Date:** 2026-03-12
**Goal:** Cast wide, find 10 problem domains worth investigating

---

## Problem Domain 1: AI-Powered Personal Knowledge Base

**The Problem:** People consume vast amounts of information (articles, videos, podcasts, books) but struggle to retain, connect, and actually *use* what they learn. Notes get scattered across apps. Search replaces understanding. The "second brain" promise hasn't delivered for most people.

**Why Now:** AI makes it possible to automatically extract connections, summarize insights, and surface relevant knowledge at the right moment. LLMs can reason across your personal corpus.

**Potential Users:** Researchers, students, professionals who read/write a lot, lifelong learners

---

## Problem Domain 2: Async Team Communication & Decision Logging

**The Problem:** Meetings proliferate because teams lack async-first communication habits. Decisions get made in Slack threads that disappear. New team members can't catch up. Context switching kills productivity.

**Why Now:** Remote/hybrid work is permanent. Tools exist but are either too async (long docs no one reads) or too sync (Slack/Teams always-on).

**Potential Users:** Remote teams, startups, distributed organizations, consultants working with multiple clients

---

## Problem Domain 3: Personal Finance for Irregular Income

**The Problem:** Traditional budgeting apps assume monthly salary. Freelancers, creators, and gig workers have unpredictable cash flow. They can't budget "per month" — they need to budget from a fluctuating pool.

**Why Now:** Creator economy growing. More people have irregular income. Existing tools (YNAB, Monarch) are designed for employees.

**Potential Users:** Freelancers, creators, contractors, small business owners

---

## Problem Domain 4: Creative Project Management for Makers

**The Problem:** Project management tools are designed for software teams. Writers, artists, musicians, designers need something that matches creative workflows — not tickets and sprints, but stages of creation, iteration, and reflection.

**Why Now:** Creative work is increasingly hybrid with digital tools. Notion/Airtable are too generic; specialized creative tools are too narrow.

**Potential Users:** Writers, filmmakers, artists, musicians, game designers, indie hackers

---

## Problem Domain 5: Spaced Repetition for Lifelong Learners

**The Problem:** Most "learning" is passive consumption. People forget 70% within 24 hours. Spaced repetition works (Anki) but has a brutal UI/UX learning curve. It's powerful but inaccessible.

**Why Now:** AI can generate optimal review schedules, adapt to individual forgetting curves, and make recall practice less tedious.

**Potential Users:** Medical students, language learners, professionals maintaining certifications, anyone wanting to remember what they learn

---

## Problem Domain 6: Local-First Privacy-Focused Productivity

**The Problem:** All your data lives in cloud services you don't control. Privacy concerns, vendor lock-in, offline limitations. People want tools that work offline, sync when online, and keep data on their devices.

**Why Now:** Privacy awareness growing. Tech stacks for local-first (CRDTs, IndexedDB) maturing. People tired of subscription fatigue.

**Potential Users:** Privacy-conscious users, journalists, researchers, people in regions with unreliable connectivity

---

## Problem Domain 7: Meeting Intelligence & Action Extraction

**The Problem:** Meetings generate notes, decisions, and tasks — but they scatter. Action items get lost. Follow-up is inconsistent. Reviewing past meetings to find decisions is painful.

**Why Now:** AI transcription is cheap and accurate. Summarization can extract key points. But most "meeting tools" focus on recording, not on the *aftermath* of meetings.

**Potential Users:** Managers, team leads, anyone who attends multiple meetings weekly

---

## Problem Domain 8: Personal CRM & Relationship Maintenance

**The Problem:** People meet lots of interesting people — at conferences, networking events, online. But they lose track. "Who was that person I met at the event? What did we talk about?" Professional networks decay without maintenance.

**Why Now:** Networking is more distributed (online + offline). AI can help remember context, suggest follow-ups, surface past conversations.

**Potential Users:** Networkers, sales professionals, founders, consultants, anyone who values relationships

---

## Problem Domain 9: Home Inventory & Asset Management

**The Problem:** People accumulate stuff. They forget what they own, where they stored it, what still works, what needs replacement. Insurance claims require documentation. Moving requires inventory. Digital asset management (photos, files) is a mess.

**Why Now:** Smart home adoption. Insurance complexity. Downsizing/upsizing moves common. AI can help categorize, search, track.

**Potential Users:** Homeowners, renters, people moving frequently, collectors, anyone with significant physical or digital assets

---

## Problem Domain 10: Digital Legacy & Life Administration

**The Problem:** What happens to your digital life when you're gone or incapacitated? Passwords, accounts, subscriptions, crypto, photos, subscriptions. Most people have no plan. It's uncomfortable to think about, but critical.

**Why Now:** Digital life is substantial. People have more "digital assets" than physical. Generational wealth transfer includes digital. Tools exist but are either too morbid or too complex.

**Potential Users:** Adults with dependents, parents, estate planners, anyone wanting control over their digital afterlife
